Executive Summary

“ At a time when the world is looking for new ways to build peace and sustainable development, people must rely on the power of intelligence to innovate, expand their horizons and sustain the hope of new humanism; for it is in the minds of men and women that the defenses of peace and the conditions for sustainable development must be built ”- UNESCO

Mission

To mentor, to educate, and to inform students on International Relations

Vision

To be a global club, active in all high school institutions

Background

This idea was born after the realization that the world through globalization has become more connected, and interactions between people, between states and between cooperates has increased. More challenges have also emerged due competition brought about by this trend. This initiative aims to equip students early enough with information on international relations, and how to identify changes in the system and adapt to them. We are also focused to improve awareness on current affairs, an allinclusive scope in political, social and economic sectors.

Over time, the world systems and theories have predicted the integration of political entries into a collaborative and cooperation mode of engagement. This has been witnessed for instance by the merger of the BRICS to attempt to bridge the divide and strengthen their position in the International system. The East Africa Community has in recent past intensified their resolve to work together and is ambitious to form a political federation in coming years. Conferences within the United Nations System are beginning to be more and more inclusive of both state and nonstate actors so as to encourage holistic and inclusive societies.

These and many other scenarios reflect the need for human beings to realize how dependent they are to one another. Ignorance is no defense and as such this project seeks to improve the alertness that currently exists to International Affairs. The world is becoming a more intimate village and consequently the need to know and understand global affairs and how they affect the man on the street becomes cardinal. Centre for International and Security Affairs’ capacity to carry out this project is driven by the quest to share and spread knowledge to the students so as bring up a young generation that is better placed to create global and local sustainable and peaceful social, economic and political solutions. Focus at the high school level is critical since this is the stage where young adults know themselves and their potentials better. The stage for careers and understanding of what International Relations means is scarce if not available at all. This is the vision of this initiative. Objectives

  • Improve readership, writing and analytical skills in the young population
  • Produce holistic, exposed and informed young population
  • Improve International Relations Awareness and Careers
  • Promote Cultural interactions
  • Create leadership and mentorship Opportunities for Students cisa-global.

Project Beneficiary

The Student

The focus of the initiative is the students, who will expand their knowledge and improve their understanding of global affairs. Once the clubs are introduced in the schools, the platform will produce leaders, scholars, diplomats, ambassadors among other possible opportunities that will presented.

The Schools

The schools administrations upon approval and cooperation, The schools will create a relationship with embassies, university students who will be mentoring the students.

The Partners

The partners who will come on board will be those that will have the interest of the students as well as the commitment to build a better generation through International Relations knowledge awareness.

Project Monitoring and Evaluation

The CISA Team as per the organogram has a structure in place to ensure the smooth periodic project progress and technical reports at the end of the school term to be a reference point; showing how the initiative can adjust to the experienced challenges to improve on its operations. These reports shall be issued to the partners and stakeholders involved upon their completion. For purposes of evaluation, CISA will consult with a Monitoring and Evaluation consultant who will give professional advice and reporting, this will improve transparency and accountability measures.
